PTSD & Trauma

Learn about post-traumatic stress disorder, its symptoms, effects, and strategies for recovery and resilience.

What is PTSD?

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Trauma can include accidents, natural disasters, abuse, war, or other life-threatening situations. PTSD can significantly impact daily functioning, relationships, and emotional well-being.

Symptoms and Signs

PTSD symptoms are often grouped into four categories:

  • Intrusive Memories: Flashbacks, nightmares, and unwanted thoughts about the traumatic event.
  • Avoidance: Avoiding places, people, or activities that remind you of the trauma.
  • Negative Mood & Cognition: Persistent fear, guilt, shame, numbness, or negative beliefs about oneself or others.
  • Altered Arousal & Reactivity: Hypervigilance, irritability, sleep difficulties, or difficulty concentrating.

Causes and Risk Factors

PTSD can develop after any traumatic event. Not everyone exposed to trauma develops PTSD, and risk factors include:

  • Severity, duration, and proximity to the traumatic event
  • Personal or family history of mental health disorders
  • Lack of social support following trauma
  • Ongoing stress or subsequent traumatic events

Treatment and Coping Strategies

Recovery from PTSD is possible with proper care. Treatment may include:

  • Therapy: Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT), Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), or exposure therapy.
  • Medication: Antidepressants or antianxiety medications may help manage symptoms.
  • Self-care & Lifestyle: Mindfulness, exercise, adequate sleep, and stress reduction techniques.
  • Support Networks: Family, friends, and peer support groups provide crucial emotional backing.

Seeking Help

If you or someone you know may be experiencing PTSD, seek professional help. Early intervention improves recovery outcomes. Licensed therapists, support groups, and crisis services are available.

Immediate Support

Contact the National Center for PTSD at 1-802-296-6300 or visit www.ptsd.va.gov for resources.
