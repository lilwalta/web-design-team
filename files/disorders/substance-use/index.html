What Are Substance Use Disorders?

Substance use disorders (SUDs) occur when the repeated use of alcohol or drugs leads to significant impairment, health issues, or inability to control use despite negative consequences.

Common Substances

  • Alcohol
  • Opioids (prescription or illicit)
  • Stimulants (cocaine, methamphetamine)
  • Cannabis
  • Prescription medications

Signs and Symptoms

  • Cravings and loss of control
  • Neglecting responsibilities
  • Withdrawal symptoms
  • Increased tolerance
  • Continued use despite harm

Treatment and Recovery

Recovery is possible and often involves a combination of:

  • Detoxification: Medically supervised withdrawal.
  • Therapy: CBT, motivational interviewing, and group counseling.
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment: Such as methadone or buprenorphine.
  • Support Groups: Peer recovery programs like AA or SMART Recovery.

When to Get Help

If substance use is affecting your health, safety, or relationships, seeking professional help can be life-saving.

Immediate Help

Call the SAMHSA National Helpline at 1-800-662-HELP (4357) or visit www.samhsa.gov.
